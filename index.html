IT Security Principles

Encrypt everything. End to end encryption for all applications.

Abandon the flat network. Segregate by geography and Function. Use firewalls and VLANS to create boundaries to contain malware and slow lateral movement by intruders (and also provide additional logging & detection opportunities)

Least Privilege. Default is RO

Data security is paramount

Defense in depth

Minimize complexity

Traffic monitoring is essential.

Disable workstation to workstation communication

It is important to understand what normal business traffic looks like and to detect any deviations from the norm. The associated ports, protocols and applications used to conduct business should be documented.

Investment Principles

There are no permanent investments

Debt is a four letter word. Avoid companies with a lot of debt

Build a portfolio of businesses with consistently very high return on invested capital

Set sell targets and adjust them regularly to reflect current information

Follow the free cash flow. Keep a lookout for companies that may be turning the corner on cash conversion related to past investments

Free cash flow subsidized by stock compensation? Particularly relevant to tech companies. Check growth in stock compensation vs growth in sales

Low operating margin by industry standards may indicate possible margin upside?

Understand what and why the company capitalizes. Look for postponement of expenses via line items such as software capitalisation on the cashflow statement

Look for discrepancies between revenue and operating income growth. Dig deeper.

Review both parties disclosures on the merger of businesses to best grasp the true economics of the transactions
